How to Evaluate (What Matters Most)

When you buy trading journal software, look beyond marketing bullets and vet how the platform fits your daily routine. Use the checkpoints below (also in the checklist) to structure demo calls and trials.

Automation & Broker Imports

List every broker and account you touch today and in the next year. Confirm the journal auto-syncs each one, captures partial fills, and offers a CSV fallback when APIs fail. Futures traders should insist on a live demo covering tick normalization and contract rollovers.

Psychology & Discipline

Choose a tool that makes pauses as easy as entries. Look for emotion prompts, rule adherence logs, and cooldown workflows that export cleanly for coaching or compliance reviews.

AI Insights vs Traditional Stats

Ask vendors to replay an AI insight and show the raw trades behind it. If you can’t verify the narrative or mute noisy prompts, the feature will become clutter. Prioritize explainable AI layered on top of the metrics you already rely on.

Performance Metrics That Actually Help Decisions

Expectancy, MAE/MFE, drawdown, streaks, and session analytics should be filterable by strategy and instrument. If a metric doesn’t change risk, sizing, or schedule decisions, it’s optional noise.

Data Portability & Ownership

Confirm exports include trades, notes, and media in open formats (CSV/PDF/Excel) with documented backups and deletion workflows. API or webhook access is the insurance policy for future analytics projects.

UX & Onboarding

Time-box onboarding. Request a sandbox, measure how long it takes to set up tags, and test support response times. If you review on mobile or offline, confirm the app or desktop companion actually works with live data.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Vanity metrics: Eye candy without direct impact on risk or execution.
  • No export plan: Lack of CSV/PDF access traps trade history when you switch.
  • Ignoring psychology: Without tilt tracking, revenge trades slip through.
  • Paying for shelfware: Skip tiers whose automation or AI you won’t deploy in 30 days.
  • Weak futures/forex support: Confirm tick normalization, rollover handling, and session analytics.

FAQs

Is auto-import necessary or is CSV enough?
Auto-imports save time and catch partial fills, but disciplined CSV uploads work for lower volumes.
How do journals handle futures ticks and rollovers?
Many automate tick normalization and rollovers—watch a demo with your symbols to confirm.
Do I need AI features to get value?
Only if the AI explains itself; otherwise lean on traditional analytics until trust is earned.
Will I lose data if I switch platforms later?
Not if you export trades, notes, and media on a schedule and keep external backups.
How long should onboarding take?
A focused afternoon should cover imports, tags, and psychology prompts—longer means the UX is too heavy.
